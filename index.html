Dan Bain is a writer, director, comedian and performance jack-of-all-trades based out of Christchurch, New Zealand. He graduated from CircoArts, the New Zealand circus training school, in 2002 and since then has toured globally as a street performer and motivational speaker, worked in three circuses, been a theatre, film and television actor, written several professionally produced plays for stage and radio and told some pretty good jokes from behind a microphone.

For your next event, festival or creative project engage over ten years of versatile professional expertise and book one of the many services of Dan Bain: Comedic Gentleman.

Corporate Entertainment

Over the last 10 years Dan has worked extensively in corporate event entertainment with The Court Jesters, Cleverfool Creative, ConArtists and The Improvisors.

He speaks to over 10,000 first year university students annually through NZ, Australia and the UK as part of the Ideas Agency's UNISMART motivational speaking programme.

While he works through a number of agents for the majority of his corporate work, Dan personally offers these unique products for your conference or seminar.

Public Speaking: Fact And Fiction

A two-hour keynote presentation given by Court Theatre CEO Philip Aldridge (ONZM), Court Theatre Artistic Director Ross Gumbley and Writer/Comedian Dan Bain.

Over the course of two hours, three of Christchurch's most qualified practitioners entertain, inform and debunk popular myths on presentation and public speaking, giving insights and lessons learnt from the the rehearsal room, the board room and the writers room cupboard of New Zealand's most successful professional theatre company.

Ideal for management, sales teams or anyone who's had to stand in front of a group of people and convince them of something.

Not suitable for beginners.

Children’s Theatre

After appearing in leading roles in nearly ten consecutive professional kids’ shows at the Court Theatre, Dan turned his hand to writing and directing. His specialty is silent (dialogue-free) shows for children. Currently two of his original pieces are available for booking into theatre seasons and arts festivals.

For a production budget and technical requirements please contact Dan directly.

A Paintbox Of Clowns

A Paintbox of Clowns is a silent show for loud children. Come along with Red, Blue and Green as they bumble their way through an epic adventure involving slingshots, stethoscopes, ducks, carrots and retractable apples, all without saying a word.

Red is brave, Blue is smart and Green is hungry!

Why Are My Parents So Boring?

A play about playing. Unfolding in the manner of a silent movie (complete with dialogue cards), Why Are My Parents So Boring? explores the relationship between a hyper-active seven year old and her parents. Who are soooo boring…

Off-beat and heartwarming, Why Are My Parents so Boring? is must see live children's entertainment.

Gameplan

Dylan and his friends are broke and good at one thing - video games. They need 10 grand and fast, but there aren't any end of level bosses guarding treasure in real life… or are there?

What happens when a team of nerds and social rejects try to take their on-screen skills into real life? Can video games train you to be awesome? To take control of your destiny? Or to at least be able to talk to girls?

Money. Crime. Video games.

Stag Weekend (with Brendon Bennetts)

A stag party in a West Coast cabin for four overconfident and under qualified city boys becomes increasingly hilarious… and dangerous.

Stag Weekend is a 4-hander NZ comedy nearing completion. It has been in development programmes by the Court Theatre and mounted as rehearsed reading by Auckland Playwrights Collective and Spring in the Shed at the Court Theatre.
